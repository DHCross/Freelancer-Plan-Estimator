# **Resource Management**

## Enhancing Project Predictability & Resource Management

### Executive Summary

This document presents key observations from recent project execution and proposes a role-based framework to significantly improve predictability, efficiency, and resource management. By implementing targeted time allocation and mandatory tracking, we move from estimation to precise, data-driven management, thereby optimizing resource utilization and enhancing forecasting accuracy. Please note that **due to data limitations, a specific, numerical total forecasted time for a large project cannot be provided at this stage.** The focus of this document is on the allocation of time by role.

### 1\. Critical Insights from Project Analysis

Recent project analysis (notably Project A1) underscores key factors influencing timelines and resource allocation:

* **Significant Scope Dynamics**: Projects may significantly expand due to creative and mechanical necessities (e.g., Project A1 expanded from \~17k words to \>158k words). This growth, while justified, demands robust management to avoid uncontrolled scope creep.  
* **Incomplete Effort Data**: Reliance solely on individual time tracking limited accurate project cost and duration assessments. Comprehensive team-wide tracking remains essential for reliable forecasting.  
* **Process-Building Overhead**: Concurrent development of essential workflows (version control, editing sequences, layout protocols) initially added significant overhead, but this foundational infrastructure is now expected to enhance future efficiency.

### 2\. Project Scope & Role-Based Effort Allocation

Predictability improves significantly with clear scope benchmarks and structured effort distribution:

**Scope Benchmarks**:

* Micro Module: 6k‚Äì12k words  
* Small Adventure: 15k‚Äì25k words  
* Large Adventure: 50k‚Äì90k words  
* Core Rulebook: 120k‚Äì200k+ words

**Typical Role & Effort Distribution**:

* **Writing (40‚Äì50%)**: Lead drafting, secondary contributions, feedback integration.  
* **Editing (10‚Äì14%)**: Developmental editing (structure/narrative), copyediting (clarity/accuracy).  
* **Layout & Visuals (10‚Äì15%)**: Design, formatting, and final layout preparation.  
* **Project Management & Coordination (10‚Äì15%)**: Scheduling, tracking, and risk mitigation.  
* **Art/Map Coordination (3‚Äì5%)**: Asset management and vendor communications.  
* **QA/Proofreading (2‚Äì3%)**: Final quality checks.  
* **Administrative & Technical Support (10‚Äì13%)**: This includes meetings, administration, technical support, and synchronization activities.

### 3\. Structured Framework for Role-Based Management

Moving forward, predictable outcomes require clearly defined roles, allocated hours, and disciplined tracking:

**Framework Components**:

* **Defined Roles**: Explicitly assign roles per project.  
* **Hourly Allocation**: Allocate specific weekly hours per team member, project, and role.  
* **Mandatory Time Tracking**: Enforce logging of hours in dedicated tools (e.g., Clockify).  
* **Weekly Reviews**: Review planned vs. actual hours regularly, identify discrepancies, and make proactive adjustments.

**Benefits of the Framework**:

* Enhanced accuracy in forecasting  
* Improved resource allocation and utilization  
* Tightened budget control  
* Clearer visibility into project status  
* Greater operational scalability

### 4\. Implementation Guidelines

To effectively operationalize this framework:

* Log time strictly against assigned roles and projects.  
* Clearly define weekly availability for each team member.  
* Monitor progress and budgets regularly, adjusting allocations to maintain accuracy.  
* Use real-time data to refine project estimates continuously.

### 5\. Next Steps

Immediate actions to move from theory to practice:

* Assign team members specific roles and projects.  
* Confirm individual weekly hours availability.  
* Set up standardized time-tracking systems and procedures.  
* Regularly review and adjust allocations based on collected data.

### Conclusion & Recommendation

The foundational infrastructure for project production is now established. Implementing a structured role-based time allocation and tracking system is essential to fully leveraging this investment. This structured approach enables informed management decisions, tighter budget control, and enhanced project predictability.

**Recommendation**: Initiate immediate discussions to integrate this structured management framework into all current and future project workflows.

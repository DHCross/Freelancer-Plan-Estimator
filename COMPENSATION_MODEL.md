# Compensation Model Statement

## Current Structure: $20/Hour Biweekly

The current $20/hour biweekly structure is the only compensation model that has allowed this work to be financially viable for me. My household depends on predictable income to meet basic living expenses. A shift to a per-project model or any effective rate below $20/hour removes that predictability and transfers all timeline and revision risk onto me.

## Why Per-Project Pricing Does Not Work

Because the projects involve ongoing iteration, asset gaps, and editorial changes outside my control, a flat project rate would result in large amounts of unpaid time. In practice that would push my effective hourly earnings well below sustainable levels.

### Key Risk Factors in Per-Project Pricing

1. **Scope Creep** - Ongoing revisions and additions expand workload without additional compensation
2. **Asset Dependencies** - Layout work cannot proceed when waiting for art, text, or other deliverables
3. **Editorial Changes** - Revisions outside my control require rework that consumes unpaid time
4. **Timeline Risk** - All delays, regardless of cause, become my financial burden

## Position

For that reason, anything below the current $20/hour biweekly arrangement would not cover the time required to perform the work and would make it impossible for me to continue in the role.
